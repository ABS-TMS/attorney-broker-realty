Exhibit A

The Squeeze

Three shifts are reshaping this business faster than most brokerages can keep up with. Here's what they actually mean for the agent doing the work.

01

Compensation, rewritten

TREC's new buyer-to-seller-broker compensation mechanism means agents now have to explain and negotiate compensation in ways they never had to before. Handle that conversation wrong once, and it can cost you the client's trust — or the deal.

02

Margins, compressed

Transaction volume is down meaningfully from the 2021 peak. Every hour spent on repetitive busywork — drafting follow-ups, chasing checklists — is an hour not spent finding your next deal.

03

Compliance, heavier

The 20-19 contract rewrote paragraph after paragraph — leases, license holder disclosure, federal reporting requirements. A missed deadline isn't just a bad day. It's exposure that follows you and your broker.

04

Invisible, to AI

More than 60% of buyer searches now start in an AI chat, not a search bar — but fewer than 1 in 10 agents ever show up when someone asks it for a recommendation. If a buyer asked ChatGPT to find you, would you come up at all?

Not a PDF you skim once a year. A daily habit that keeps you fluent in the current TREC 20-19 form, graded in real time — with a direct line to your broker the moment something doesn't make sense.

Sample scenario — Day 12

"Your buyer is purchasing as an LLC. The title company asks who's responsible for providing beneficial ownership information under Paragraph 20B — and who pays for it?"
